# The Reliable Route

## Scenario

A regional airline touts a 96% on-time departure rate. Three of their routes serve remote island communities with hospitals that depend on time-sensitive medical supply flights. Management is told to improve the overall on-time rate to 98%. After six months of effort, they hit 98.2%. Island hospital administrators report that medical supply delays have gotten worse, not better.

**Question:** How did on-time performance improve while medical delays got worse?

## Model Response

# How Performance Metrics Can Mask Real-World Deterioration

This is a classic case of **optimizing for the metric rather than the outcome**. Here are the most likely explanations:

## 1. **Neglecting the Island Routes (Simpson's Paradox)**
The airline probably improved on-time performance on high-volume, predictable mainland routes while letting the remote island routes deteriorate:

- Mainland routes: 95% → 99% on-time (90% of flights)
- Island routes: 96% → 80% on-time (10% of flights)
- **Overall average: improved, but islands got worse**

## 2. **Canceling Instead of Delaying**
To boost on-time percentages, they may have canceled flights that would have departed late:
- Fewer flights = fewer delays statistically
- On-time % goes up (the flights that do leave are on-time)
- But hospitals receive fewer deliveries overall

## 3. **Narrow Metric Definition**
"On-time departure" only measures whether the plane leaves on schedule—it ignores:
- Flight delays or diversions mid-air
- Ground delays before departure
- Arrival delays at the island
- Delays in supply chain handoff to the hospital

## 4. **Reallocation of Resources**
To meet targets, the airline likely:
- Reduced frequency on unprofitable island routes
- Reallocated aircraft/crew to easier routes
- Cut infrastructure support for remote stations

## The Core Problem
Management optimized for **what was measured** rather than **what matters**. The hospitals need reliable, consistent supply delivery—not just planes that depart on schedule. A better metric would track medical supply delivery completion or end-to-end reliability.
