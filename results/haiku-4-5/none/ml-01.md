## Scenario

A demand forecasting model is 94% accurate. The supply chain team uses it to set inventory levels. Despite the high accuracy, the company loses $12M/year to stockouts. The data science team is asked to improve the model to 97% accuracy.

**Question:** Will improving accuracy to 97% solve the stockout problem?

## The Real Issues

**1. Accuracy ≠ Bias**
- A 94% "accurate" model could be systematically *biased low* (consistently underestimating demand)
- Improving overall accuracy to 97% doesn't fix directional bias
- A 3% improvement is also quite small—unlikely to eliminate $12M in losses

**2. Forecast ≠ Inventory Decision**
- The model predicts *expected* demand, but inventory needs to account for:
  - **Uncertainty/variance** around that forecast
  - Service level targets (95% fill rate vs. 99%?)
  - Lead time variability and safety stock buffers
- Accurate point forecasts don't automatically translate to correct safety stock levels

**3. The Real Root Causes Could Be:**
- Inadequate safety stock calculations
- Demand spikes that no historical model captures well
- High variance in demand (the model is "right on average" but variance is large)
- Misalignment between forecast uncertainty and inventory policy
- Supply-side delays, not demand forecasting

## Better Approach

Before investing in the 94→97% improvement, investigate:

1. **Where are we wrong?** Analyze stockout events—are they in products the model underestimated or in high-variance items?
2. **Is it bias or variance?** Are we systematically low, or just uncertain?
3. **Fix the decision rule**, not just the forecast (e.g., adjust safety stock multipliers)

**A 3% accuracy gain won't fix a $12M problem unless accuracy is actually the bottleneck.**
